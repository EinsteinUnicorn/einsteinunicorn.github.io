Welcome to My Portfolio

Hi, I'm Becky Button. I'm a maker and a graduate of the Electrical and Computer Engineering program at Carnegie Mellon University. I have over 10 years of experience building projects with microncontrollers, 3D printers and various other emerging technologies. Like many other people, I enjoy doing things that are both fun to do, and sometimes useful for other people. So far that that venture has taken me to the world of small scale manufacturing, assistive technology, semiconductor fabrication, musical effects and probably a few other things I'm forgetting. Please check out some of my projects below, and if you would like to connect, please reach out to me at beckybutton@proton.me. I'm always looking for opportunities to collaborate and help others on projects.

My Projects

Purple Pedal Project

Developed a reprogrammable digital effects pedal and curriculum in collaboration with Pittsburgh Sound Preserve, Carnegie Mellon, Jason Garwood and Rowan Dunlop. Photo Credit: Jason Garwood

HackerFab

I worked in the HackerFab, the first open source Semiconductor fab built at Carnegie Mellon. My work was focused on electrical characterization of our process, and characterization of the thin film on the chips.

Twinkle Toes

Developed a new take on reprogrammable effects pedal in collaboration with PAS Berlin, XHain, and Winny Li.

DigiBraille

Developed a web connected Braille Embosser in a Team with Joshna Iyengar, Zeynep Ozkaya and user study participants at the Library of Accessible Media in Pittsburgh.

Bioprinting Research

Developed the Bioprinting process at Virginia Tech based on Jordan Miller's bioprinting work. Learned various biological material protocols + methods, and learned to image using the confocal machine.

WiFi Deauthing Sandals

Created a pair of wifi deauthing sandals with Naomi Wu.

Maker Movement and 3D Printing Advocacy

Went to events, lead workshops, presented at panels, and wrote op-eds advocating for Maker Education, 3D printing, and democratizing access to these things to more people

Bioinformatics Research

Developed an machine learning method to track cells as they went through growth phases. Created a database of transcriptomics and phenotype data. Work was publised in a journal.

E-Nable - 3D printing & designing Prosthetics

Active member of the E-Nable community. Developed a myoelectric prosthetic that utilized a cheaper sensor that worked well for those with amniotic band syndrome that still had a functional elbow.

HackerFab Details

During my time at the HackerFab, I worked to go through pieces of the process, which included testing our Spin On Glass process, testing the electrical properties of the traces, and using spectroscopy methods to determine how thick the spin on glass was. I designed 3D printed spectrometer mounts for both the microscope and the plasma etcher.

Purple Pedal Details

Developed a low-cost reprogrammable effects pedal to bring to Pittsburgh's Open Improv Lab. Lead workshops at NYC Resistor, Coney Island Maker Faire, Hackaday Supercon, and Hackaday Berlin. PCBway, and FlashPCB generously donated PCBS for workshops. The pedal was designed in collaboration with Jason Garwood, who designed the enclosure and educational materials.

Twinkle Toes Details

While doing a residency at Petersburg Art Space Berlin, I developed a foot shaped effects pedal. Winny Li developed the graphic design for the pedal. I had several workshops across Germany, included at the FSCK hacker camp and with people I met at Superbooth. Aisler sponsored the PCB's for the workshops.

DigiBraille Details

I designed the mechanical parts of our webconnected braille embosser. Did a focus group with blind users to verify the design was actually useful for the people we intended to use it. Added textures on the surfaces to ensure peole would know how to orient the paper.
